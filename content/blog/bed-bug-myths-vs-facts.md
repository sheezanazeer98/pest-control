Bed bugs are one of the most misunderstood pests in America. Misconceptions about these tiny hitchhikers can lead homeowners to make costly mistakes or delay getting proper treatment. Let's separate fact from fiction.

## Myth #1: Bed Bugs Only Live in Dirty Homes

**FACT:** Bed bugs don't discriminate based on cleanliness. They've been found in five-star hotels, spotless apartments, and well-maintained homes. Bed bugs are attracted to warmth, carbon dioxide, and blood — not dirt.

While clutter can give bed bugs more places to hide, a perfectly clean home can absolutely have a bed bug problem.

## Myth #2: Bed Bugs Are Too Small to See

**FACT:** Adult bed bugs are about the size of an apple seed (4-5mm) and are visible to the naked eye. They're flat, oval-shaped, and reddish-brown in color. Nymphs (juveniles) are smaller and lighter but still visible, especially after feeding.

What's harder to see are their eggs, which are tiny, white, and about 1mm long.

## Myth #3: Bed Bugs Only Bite at Night

**FACT:** While bed bugs are primarily nocturnal and prefer to feed while you sleep, they will bite during the day if they're hungry enough. This is especially true in severe infestations or in darkened rooms during daytime hours.

## Myth #4: Bed Bugs Transmit Diseases

**FACT:** Despite being blood-feeding parasites, bed bugs are **not known to transmit diseases** to humans. However, their bites can cause:

- Itchy, red welts that can become infected from scratching
- Allergic reactions in some people
- Significant psychological distress and insomnia
- Secondary skin infections

## Myth #5: You Can Get Rid of Bed Bugs With DIY Methods

**FACT:** While some DIY methods can help manage minor issues, bed bugs are extremely difficult to eliminate without professional treatment. Here's why:

- They hide in tiny crevices that sprays can't reach
- They can survive months without feeding
- Over-the-counter pesticides often don't work against bed bug populations that have developed resistance
- Improperly applied treatments can scatter bugs to new areas of your home

Professional heat treatments and targeted chemical applications are the most effective solutions.

## Myth #6: Throwing Away Your Mattress Will Solve the Problem

**FACT:** Bed bugs don't just live in mattresses. They hide in:

- Box springs and bed frames
- Nightstands and dressers
- Baseboards and carpet edges
- Electrical outlets
- Picture frames and wall hangings
- Upholstered furniture
- Luggage and clothing

Throwing away your mattress without treating these other areas just wastes money and leaves the real problem in place.

## Myth #7: Bed Bugs Jump or Fly

**FACT:** Bed bugs cannot jump or fly. They crawl, and they're surprisingly fast — about 4 feet per minute. They spread primarily by hitchhiking on luggage, clothing, used furniture, and other items.

## How to Actually Prevent Bed Bugs

Now that we've cleared up the myths, here are real prevention strategies:

1. **Inspect hotel rooms** before unpacking — check the mattress seams, headboard, and nightstand
2. **Keep luggage on hard surfaces** (not the bed or floor) when traveling
3. **Wash and dry travel clothes on high heat** immediately when you return home
4. **Inspect used furniture carefully** before bringing it into your home
5. **Use mattress encasements** to reduce hiding spots
6. **Reduce clutter** around beds and sleeping areas
