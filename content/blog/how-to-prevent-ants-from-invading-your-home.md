As temperatures rise in Northeastern Pennsylvania, ants become one of the most common household invaders. From tiny sugar ants trailing across your kitchen counter to carpenter ants tunneling through your wood structures, these persistent pests can turn a peaceful home into a frustrating battleground.

At EcoShield Pest Control, we treat ant infestations throughout NEPA every spring and summer. Here's how to keep them out in the first place.

## Why Ants Invade in Spring

Ant colonies spend winter in a dormant state underground. When the soil warms up — typically in late March through April in our area — scout ants venture out searching for two things: **food** and **water**.

Your home provides both in abundance, which is why kitchens and bathrooms are the most common entry points.

## 7 Proven Prevention Strategies

### 1. Seal Entry Points

Ants can squeeze through cracks as thin as a credit card. Inspect your home's exterior and seal:

- Gaps around windows and doors
- Cracks in the foundation
- Openings where pipes and wires enter
- Spaces around vents and utility boxes

Use silicone caulk for most gaps and expanding foam for larger openings.

### 2. Eliminate Food Sources

Ants are attracted to sugary, greasy, and protein-rich foods. Keep them at bay by:

- Storing food in airtight containers
- Cleaning up crumbs and spills immediately
- Wiping down counters and stovetops daily
- Taking out garbage regularly
- Keeping pet food dishes clean

### 3. Remove Standing Water

Many ant species are attracted to moisture. Fix leaky faucets, ensure proper drainage around your foundation, and use a dehumidifier in damp areas like basements.

### 4. Trim Vegetation Away From Your Home

Branches and shrubs touching your house create bridges for ants. Maintain a clear zone of at least 12 inches between landscaping and your home's exterior walls.

### 5. Keep Firewood Away From the House

Stack firewood at least 20 feet from your home and elevate it off the ground. Carpenter ants are especially attracted to damp or decaying wood.

### 6. Clean Up Outdoor Dining Areas

If you eat on a patio or deck, clean up thoroughly afterward. Even small food residue can attract thousands of ants within hours.

### 7. Address Moisture Issues in Crawl Spaces

Damp crawl spaces are magnets for multiple ant species. Ensure proper ventilation and consider installing a vapor barrier.

## When to Call a Professional

DIY prevention works well for keeping ants at bay, but if you're already seeing ant trails in your home, you may be dealing with an established colony nearby. Signs you need professional help include:

- Seeing ants consistently despite cleaning efforts
- Finding winged ants indoors (indicates a mature colony)
- Discovering piles of sawdust-like material (carpenter ant damage)
- Multiple ant trails in different areas of your home
