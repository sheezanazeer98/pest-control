Mosquitoes and ticks aren't just annoying — they're dangerous. Mosquitoes can transmit West Nile virus, while ticks in Northeastern Pennsylvania carry Lyme disease, anaplasmosis, and other serious illnesses. Protecting your yard is protecting your family's health.

Here's how to create a safer outdoor environment.

## Understanding the Threat

### Mosquitoes in NEPA

The most common disease-carrying mosquitoes in our region are the *Aedes* and *Culex* species. They breed in standing water and are most active at dawn and dusk.

### Ticks in NEPA

The blacklegged tick (deer tick) is the primary carrier of Lyme disease in Pennsylvania. PA consistently ranks among the top states for Lyme disease cases, with thousands reported annually.

## 8 Ways to Reduce Mosquitoes in Your Yard

### 1. Eliminate Standing Water

Mosquitoes need just a bottle cap of water to breed. Walk your property weekly and dump, drain, or treat:

- Flower pot saucers
- Bird baths (change water twice weekly)
- Gutters and downspout extensions
- Tarps and pool covers
- Old tires and containers
- Children's toys that collect water

### 2. Maintain Your Lawn

Keep grass mowed short. Mosquitoes rest in tall grass and vegetation during the heat of the day.

### 3. Plant Natural Repellents

Certain plants help deter mosquitoes:

- Citronella
- Lavender
- Marigolds
- Basil
- Rosemary

While these won't eliminate mosquitoes, they can reduce activity in immediate areas.

### 4. Install or Repair Window Screens

Make sure all windows and doors have properly fitted screens without holes or gaps.

### 5. Use Outdoor Fans

Mosquitoes are weak fliers. A good outdoor fan on your patio or deck can significantly reduce mosquito activity in that area.

### 6. Keep Gutters Clean

Clogged gutters create perfect mosquito breeding pools. Clean them at least twice a year.

### 7. Treat Water Features

If you have a pond or water garden, use mosquito dunks (Bti) to prevent larvae development without harming fish or plants.

### 8. Consider Professional Barrier Treatments

Professional mosquito barrier sprays create a protective zone around your yard that lasts for weeks. This is the most effective approach for consistent protection.

## 6 Ways to Reduce Ticks in Your Yard

### 1. Create a Tick-Safe Zone

Maintain a 3-foot-wide barrier of gravel or wood chips between your lawn and any wooded areas. Ticks rarely cross dry, hot surfaces.

### 2. Keep Leaves Raked

Ticks thrive in leaf litter. Keep your yard clear of fallen leaves, especially in areas where family members play or relax.

### 3. Stack Firewood Neatly in Dry Areas

Messy woodpiles attract mice, which are a primary host for the ticks that carry Lyme disease.

### 4. Remove Yard Debris

Old furniture, mattresses, and other junk provide habitat for tick-carrying rodents.

### 5. Consider Tick Tubes

Tick tubes are a targeted solution that treats mice nests with a tick-killing compound. Mice use the treated cotton for nesting material, which kills ticks on the mice without harming other wildlife.

### 6. Treat High-Traffic Areas

Professional tick treatments applied to yard perimeters, play areas, and garden borders can dramatically reduce tick populations.

## Personal Protection Tips

In addition to yard management:

- Wear light-colored clothing when in wooded areas (makes ticks easier to spot)
- Tuck pants into socks when hiking
- Use EPA-registered insect repellents
- Check yourself, children, and pets for ticks after outdoor activities
- Shower within 2 hours of being outdoors
