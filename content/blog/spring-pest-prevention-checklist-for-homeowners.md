Spring in Northeastern Pennsylvania is beautiful — but it also signals the start of pest season. As snow melts and temperatures climb, insects and rodents that were dormant all winter become active and start searching for food, water, and shelter.

The good news? A little prevention goes a long way. Use this comprehensive checklist to pest-proof your home before problems start.

## Exterior Checklist

### Foundation & Walls

- [ ] Inspect foundation for cracks and gaps — seal with caulk or concrete patch
- [ ] Check where utilities enter the home — seal gaps around pipes, wires, and cables
- [ ] Repair or replace damaged weatherstripping around doors
- [ ] Install door sweeps on exterior doors
- [ ] Inspect siding for gaps, rot, or damage
- [ ] Check weep holes in brick for insect screens

### Roof & Attic

- [ ] Inspect roof for damaged or missing shingles
- [ ] Check soffit vents and attic vents for gaps or damage
- [ ] Install chimney caps if missing
- [ ] Trim tree branches back at least 3 feet from the roof
- [ ] Look for signs of wildlife activity (droppings, nesting material, damaged insulation)

### Yard & Landscape

- [ ] Remove leaf litter and fallen debris
- [ ] Rake mulch away from foundation (keep at least 6 inches clear)
- [ ] Trim shrubs and bushes so they don't touch exterior walls
- [ ] Stack firewood 20+ feet from the house, elevated off the ground
- [ ] Clean and repair gutters and downspouts
- [ ] Eliminate standing water sources (pots, tarps, old tires, toys)
- [ ] Repair or clean bird baths

### Outdoor Structures

- [ ] Inspect sheds and outbuildings for pest entry points
- [ ] Check deck and porch areas for wood damage (termites, carpenter ants)
- [ ] Inspect outdoor furniture for spider webs and egg sacs
- [ ] Clean outdoor cooking and eating areas thoroughly

## Interior Checklist

### Kitchen

- [ ] Deep clean behind and under appliances
- [ ] Transfer open food packages to sealed containers
- [ ] Fix leaky faucets and pipes
- [ ] Clean out pantry and check for expired food
- [ ] Empty and clean garbage cans
- [ ] Check under sink for moisture or signs of pests

### Bathrooms

- [ ] Fix any leaky pipes or faucets
- [ ] Ensure exhaust fans are working (reduces moisture)
- [ ] Check for gaps around pipes under sinks
- [ ] Clean drains to remove organic buildup

### Basement & Crawl Space

- [ ] Inspect for moisture issues — use a dehumidifier if needed
- [ ] Check for cracks in walls and floor
- [ ] Look for termite mud tubes along walls and piers
- [ ] Ensure vapor barrier is intact (crawl spaces)
- [ ] Declutter and organize storage (use plastic bins, not cardboard)

### General Interior

- [ ] Vacuum regularly, especially along baseboards and under furniture
- [ ] Inspect window screens for holes — repair or replace
- [ ] Check around windows for gaps in caulking
- [ ] Keep pet areas clean — wash bedding regularly
- [ ] Store seasonal clothing in sealed containers

## Seasonal Treatment Plan

For the best protection, pair your prevention efforts with a professional pest management plan:

| Season | Focus Areas |
|--------|-------------|
| **Spring** | General pest barrier treatment, termite inspection, ant prevention |
| **Summer** | Mosquito/tick barrier sprays, wasp nest removal, ongoing monitoring |
| **Fall** | Rodent exclusion, stink bug prevention, entry point sealing |
| **Winter** | Interior monitoring, rodent bait stations, moisture management |

## The Bottom Line

Prevention is always cheaper and easier than treatment. By spending a few hours on this checklist each spring, you can avoid the stress, expense, and health risks of a pest infestation.
